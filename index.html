The Jigawa State Project

Nigerian women have a 1 in 20 chance of dying from postpartum hemorrhaging (PPH); PPH is the leading cause of maternal mortality globally.


Misoprostol is a cheap, oral pill that can save 85-90% of PPH cases. The World Health Organization (WHO) recommends misoprostol as the ideal uterotonic to prevent PPH in low-resource environments.

Pillars of our Work:

Scaling Existing Solutions. We believe the issue of poor maternal health is more a cause of poor implementation than poor solutions. The solutions exist; they're just not accessible to those who need them the most.

Upskilling Health Workers. Health systems are only as good as their human capacity. Maternal deaths, coincidentally, occur more frequently in health systems with low human capacity.

Building with Communities . We have an advisory board of policy makers, non-profit leaders and medical professionals, many with relevant local experience.

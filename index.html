The Problem

830 Women Die Daily. 20% from Nigeria.

The number of dots to the left is how many women will die today. Globally, 50% of births happen with unskilled birth attendants - many are illiterate. 66% of maternal deaths happen in Africa - and 22% in South Asia. Women lack access to medication, safe birthing facilities and skilled birth attendants. This contributes to 300,000+ maternal mortality cases annually.

The Leading Issue

PPH Causes 25-60% of Maternal Deaths

Postpartum hemorrhaging (PPH) is the #1 cause of maternal mortality. Globally, it accounts for 1/3 of maternal deaths, but some developing areas report figures as high as 60%. PPH happens after delivery - it's when a women bleeds to death . The 2 most common reasons PPH happens is uterine atony or uterine trauma. (This just means the uterus is too tired to remove the placenta). Utertonic drugs like oxytocin are the gold-standard of PPH treatment.

The Causes

Maternal Mortality Happens for 3 Reasons.

It boils down to lack-of-access. The 3 delay framework describes why women don't reach the treatment they need:

  1. 1. A delay in seeking care
  2. 2. A delay in transporting to care
  3. 3. A delay in recieing quality care
3 Delays for Uterotonics

Why Women Lack Access to Uterotonics:

1. 70-75% of women in Nigeria use unskilled birth attendants. They don't recognize the vital signs of PPH. (delay in seeking)


2. Hospitals are located 10s of kilometres away. Villages lack cars and proper road infrastructure. (delay in transporting)


3.Hospitals are understaffed and understocked. Drugs like oxytocin are normally unavailable. (delay in recieving quality care)

Challenges

Affordability & Culture are the Underlying Barriers

It's the poorest women who are dying. Located in remote or rural locations. Often iliterate and not educated. Social norms around women - like that strong women have many children - create a stigma around seeking for medical care.

We can change the status quo. Here are our active projects:

Non-Doctor PPH Training

We are building a pictorial training curriculum to educate any untrained birth attendants. This training covers the basics of PPH. It also introduces misoprostol, the best uterotonic drug for low-resource environments

Learn More

Misoprostol in Jigawa, Nigeria

Misoprostol is a uterotonic drug. We are working with FAYOHI, a local nonprofit, and the Jigawa State Government to make misoprostol physically available in villages at an affordable cost.
